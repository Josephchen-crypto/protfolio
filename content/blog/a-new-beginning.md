## A New Beginning

I made the decision around June 23–25. I'm going to team up with my brother and sister and turn this into a family business.

I sat with it for a long time before committing.

Walking away from a decade of building software, demoting it from career to hobby — that part still stings. Fifteen years of late nights, and my body paid the price. But the choice was clear. I questioned myself, wondered if I was just bad at it. I wasn't — at my last job, I largely single-handedly shipped a major project from zero to production.

The real reason was simpler: my age and energy couldn't keep up with the pace of the work anymore. And looking ahead, AI is already replacing programming work, and any architecture or text-based reasoning can be done by AI too. By the time that shift fully lands, it's too late to pivot. So I'm stepping out of the tech track now, while the path isn't completely blocked.

It feels wasteful at first — a decade of my life, set down. But the blame is mine. I chased techniques instead of staying in one industry and going deep. That choice brought me here.

Lesson learned.

The honest part: my body is worn down, and at this age, I don't have the stamina to match the years. The window for quick money has closed. But through patient, steady accumulation, I believe I'll get back on my feet and climb again.

I forgot to mention — the industry I'm stepping into is the one I wrote about before: textile trading, together with my brother and sister. A new path, a new beginning, and with it, a new sense of possibility.

Rest well. Rebuild the body. Focus on the work. Read and learn. Things will get better.
